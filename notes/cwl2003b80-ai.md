Feelings and summary:

This report explores the technical competencies and conceptual shifts required to transition from a Web2 consumer to a Web3 power user. Throughout the training modules, we focused on establishing a persistent digital identity via the Ethereum Name Service (ENS), executing decentralized financial transactions, and analyzing the inherent portability of user inventory across the Ethereum ecosystem.

Beyond simple registration, a critical technical milestone involved configuring the Reverse Record, or the Primary ENS Name. This configuration allows decentralized applications to resolve an Ethereum address back to its human-readable counterpart, facilitating a personalized interface that persists across disconnected platforms. On a conceptual level, this process transforms a wallet from a cold, mathematical utility into a sovereign digital character. It creates a sense of "personhood" on-chain that operates entirely independent of centralized authorities.

Interacting with the blockchain requires a shift in how we view digital permissions. We analyzed this through the lens of execution logic, where every on-chain action—from naming a wallet to swapping assets—requires a "sovereign signature." In practice, this means "throwing a transaction over the wall" to a browser extension like MetaMask.

Our analysis verified the robustness of this security architecture; private keys and mnemonics remain encrypted within the extension and are never exposed to the application’s front-end. This design fosters a high degree of user agency, as the requirement for an explicit signature ensures the user remains the ultimate arbiter of all state changes on the blockchain. It effectively moves the power balance from the service provider back to the individual.

The shift from centralized order-book exchanges to decentralized protocols like Uniswap introduces the concept of an "unstoppable vending machine." Unlike traditional entities, these decentralized exchanges utilize smart contracts that manage liquidity reserves of token pairs at specific ratios.

In today's mission, we also need to test to transfer to classmates to test the logic.

A defining characteristic of the Web3 ecosystem is that data and inventory are held by the user rather than the application. By utilizing third-party analytics tools such as [Zapper.fi](http://Zapper.fi), we demonstrated that a user’s entire inventory—including ETH, stablecoins, and NFTs—follows them instantly to any platform they visit.

While some assets like DAI may require manual "importing" into a wallet interface to be visible, the underlying ownership remains constant. This creates a seamless and unified internet experience. The broader realization is that a user’s history and assets are no longer siloed within individual websites; instead, they exist as a permanent, portable part of a digital identity that the user carries across the entire web.

To combat the volatile nature of the cryptocurrency market, which often results in a poor user experience, we integrated the use of stablecoins. We specifically analyzed DAI, an over-collateralized asset that maintains its peg to the US Dollar through a sophisticated system of minting, burning, and collateralized debt positions.

From a user perspective, utilizing stablecoins is essential for professional commerce. It mitigates the "punch to the gut" feeling of watching one's assets lose significant value due to market fluctuations. By providing price stability, these assets offer the predictability necessary for long-term financial planning and everyday transactions within a decentralized framework.

Graduating from a passive consumer to a Web3 power user requires a fundamental mastery of self-custody, gas mechanics, and smart contract logic. By establishing a human-readable identity and embracing decentralized liquidity protocols, individuals can navigate the digital economy with a level of transparency and sovereignty that was previously impossible in the centralized web.
